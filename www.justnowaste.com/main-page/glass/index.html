Glass

lass recycling is the processing of waste glass into usable products. Glass that is crushed and ready to be remelted is called cullet.There are two types of cullet: internal and external. Internal cullet is composed of defective products detected and rejected by a quality control process during the industrial process of glass manufacturing, transition phases of product changes (such as thickness and colour changes) and production offcuts.

Wine glass

Wine glass is a type of glass that is used to drink and taste wine

Recyclable

-Yellow-lid wheelie bin

Hazardous substances

The basic glass components are non-toxic, but in the process of production, it is inevitable to mix harmful substances.

Impact on Environment

In the process of production, glass will inevitably be mixed with heavy metal elements, especially brightly colored glass. This glass may contain harmful or even highly toxic elements such as lead, chromium, cadmium, nickel, copper, manganese and other heavy metals. Irregularly treated glass products may dissolve harmful elements, pollute water sources and soil, causing environmental pollution.

Broken Glass

The broken glass products

Non-recyclable

-Red-lid wheelie bin

Hazardous substances

The basic glass components are non-toxic, but in the process of production, it is inevitable to mix harmful substances.

Impact on Environment

Since broken glass is not easily decomposed by microorganisms, it causes long-term environmental pollution. Glass crumbs, like waste plastics, are pollutants that have remained in the environment for decades or even centuries. Once they enter the environment, they are not degraded by wind, rain or microbial action. Broken glass has become one of the most difficult public substances in the world. In many areas, a large amount of glass swarf is scattered and accumulated in the soil, causing serious environmental pollution.
